I am a Research Scientist in the Department of Computer Science at Stanford University, working with Prof. Jure Leskovec at the SNAP group. I am also an instructor for CS224W (Machine Learning with Graphs) and CS246 (Mining Massive Datasets).

Before Stanford, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Electrical and Systems Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania, working with Prof. Alejandro Ribeiro. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Minnesota, advised by Prof. Nikos Sidiropoulos. Earlier, I earned my Diploma in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the National Technical University of Athens.

Research interests. I build foundation models for structured data — relational databases, graphs, single-cell transcriptomics, and protein sequences — and develop parameter-efficient methods for adapting large pretrained models. My recent work spans graph neural networks and graph foundation models, multi-modal language and knowledge-graph models, structured large-model pretraining, relational AI-agents, and biomedical AI. I lead industry-academic collaborations with NVIDIA, Microsoft, SAP, Kumo.AI, and the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative.

Get in touch. Email is the best way — charilaos@cs.stanford.edu. You can also find me on Google Scholar, GitHub, and LinkedIn.
